Previous Work & Projects

This section is a collection of projects that I found interesting or notable to my own growth as an engineer. They vary in a range of topics from natural language processing research to functional, deployed enterprise software features.

01. Time Anomalies

Time Anomalies was a feature that I worked for over 1.5 years at Workday. The first machine learning infused feature in our product pillar, Time Anomalies allowed managers and administrators to find anomalous instances of worker’s time.

I was responsible for the application and data collection layer, which we ultimately deployed to over 50 (and counting) live Workday customers.

02. Air Now

AirNow was a final project for one of my systems courses at the University of Texas Austin. Primarily written in Kotlin, Google’s android development language based on Java, it utilized the AirVisual and Google Maps API to allow users to lookup and store air quality.

The application also used Firestore, Google’s no-SQL database to store user authentication information and saved air quality locations. Currently, I’m working to port this over to Flutter, a cross-platform language that offers iOS and Android support.

03. Adversarial Attacks

Pre-trained transformer-based machine learning networks are used in applications ranging from language translation to text generation like that used by Open AI’s GPT3.

In this research paper, I examine the performance of a popular pre-trained transformer network known as Electra and anaylze the performance on Stanford’s SQuAD dataset and adversarial attacks.
